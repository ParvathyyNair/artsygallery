Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the sun:
Size: Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's moon.
Orbit: Mercury orbits the sun every 88 Earth days, making it the fastest planet in the solar system.
Temperature: Mercury's surface temperature varies from 400°C during the day to -170°C at night.
Atmosphere: Mercury has no atmosphere to retain heat.
Gravity: If you weigh 100 pounds on Earth, you'd weigh 38 pounds on Mercury.
Name: Mercury is named after the Roman messenger god, who was known for being swift-footed.
Discovery: Mercury was first observed through telescopes in the 17th century by Galileo Galilei and Thomas Harriot.
